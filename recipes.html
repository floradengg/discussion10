Swedish Pancakes

Origin: Sweden

Source: Kristi, true-north-kitchen.com

Category: Breakfast


Recipe Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Vanilla
  • Milk
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Flour

Recipe Steps

  1. Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until completely smooth.Transfer to the refrigerator to rest for at least 2 hours or up to 2 days.
  2. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees if you plan to eat the pannkakor right away.
  3. Heat a 10 inch nonstick skillet over medium heat. Stir the batter briefly to recombine.
  4. Brush skillet with melted butter. Pick the skillet up off of the burner and quickly add 1⁄4 cup of batter.
  5. Immediately begin swirling the batter around the skillet so that it coats the bottom of the pan. Continue swirling until the batter is just set. Return skillet to the burner.
  6. Cook until the pancake is golden brown in spots on the bottom, about 30 seconds. Use a thin spatula to loosen the pancake, flip it over continue to cook on the other side for about 30 seconds.
  7. Transfer to a plate. Cover with foil and transfer to oven to keep warm and repeat with remaining batter, wiping out skillet in between pancakes with paper towel as needed.
  8. Serve with lingonberry jam and butter (see note below) or other desired toppings and/or fillings. See notes below for more information about making ahead of time and storing in the refrigerator or freezer.

Double Chocolate Cookies

Origin: Michigan

Source: Family Recipe

Category: Dessert

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Packed light or dark brown sugar
  • Large egg
  • Pure vanilla extract
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks (melted)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks

Recipe Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. In a separate bowl combine the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder and salt
  5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients
  6. Add the unmelted chocolate chunks.
  7. Form 15 cookies and place on a baking sheet.
  8. Cook for 12 to 13 minutes at 350 degrees.

Chinese Hot and Sour Soup

Origin: China

Source: family recipe

Category: appetizer


Recipe Ingredients

  • chicken broth
  • cornstarch
  • mushrooms
  • eggs
  • green onions

  2. Bring the soup to a simmer
  3. Add the remaining stock, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, soy sauce
  4. Slowly drizzle the whisked eggs into the soup
